English proverbs with their meaning!

15.All roads lead to Rome. 16.Better late than never.

1.A bad excuse is better than none.

Meanings:

Always give an excuse, even if it's a poor one.



2.A bad tree does not yield good apples.

Meanings:

A bad parent does not raise good children.



3.A bad workman blames his tools.

Meanings:

Blaming the tools for bad workmanship is an excuse for lack of skill.



4.A change is as good as a rest.

Meanings:

A change in routine is often as refreshing as a break or a holiday.



5.A friend in need is a friend indeed.

Meanings:

Someone who helps you when you are in trouble is a real friend.



6.A friend's eye is a good mirror.

Meanings:

A real friend will tell you the truth.



7.A good beginning makes a good end.

Meanings:

If a task is carefully planned, there's a better chance that it will be well done.



8.A loaded wagon makes no noise.

Meanings:

Really wealthy people do not talk about money.



9.A young idler, an old beggar.

Meanings:

If you don't work, you won't have any money when you're old.



10.All in good time.

Meanings:

Be patient.Things will happen when the time is right.



11.A wise head keeps a still tongue.

Meanings:

An intelligent person knows when to stay silent.



12.A happy heart is better than a full purse.

Meanings:

Happiness is better than wealth.



13.All is fair in love and war.

Meanings:

Things that are done in love or war can often be excused.



14.Better to drink the milk than to eat the cow.

Meanings:

Be careful not to destroy the source of your income or welfare.



15.All roads lead to Rome.

Meanings:

There are many different ways to reach an objective.



16.Better late than never.

Meanings:

It's better to do something, even if it's late, than not do it at all.
